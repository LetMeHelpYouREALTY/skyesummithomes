Understanding Las Vegas Zip Codes

The Las Vegas Valley spans over 600 square miles across Clark County, Nevada, encompassing six distinct municipalities and dozens of master-planned communities. Whether you're relocating to Southern Nevada, investing in real estate, or simply exploring neighborhoods, understanding the zip code geography is essential for making an informed decision.

Las Vegas (89101–89166)

The City of Las Vegas proper covers the widest range of zip codes in the valley. Downtown (89101) anchors the urban core with the Arts District, Fremont East, and Symphony Park. Moving west, you'll find established neighborhoods like The Lakes (89117), Peccole Ranch (89145), and the master-planned community of Summerlin spanning multiple zip codes (89128, 89134, 89135, 89138, 89144). The southwest corridor — 89113, 89139, 89141, 89148, 89178 — includes Southern Highlands, Rhodes Ranch, and Mountain's Edge, among the valley's fastest-growing areas.

Henderson (89002, 89011–89015, 89044, 89052, 89074)

Nevada's second-largest city occupies the southeastern valley with some of the region's most desirable master-planned communities. Green Valley (89012, 89014, 89052, 89074) pioneered the master-planned concept in Southern Nevada. Henderson also includes Lake Las Vegas (89011), Cadence, MacDonald Ranch, Anthem, and Sun City — offering everything from luxury estate homes to active-adult living. Boulder City (89005), while technically a separate municipality, is often grouped with the Henderson corridor.

North Las Vegas (89030–89086)

North Las Vegas has experienced dramatic growth, particularly in the Aliante (89031) and Tule Springs (89084, 89085) corridors. The city offers some of the valley's most competitive pricing with new construction from major national builders. Skye Canyon and the surrounding 89166 area represent the newest frontier of development in the northwest valley.
