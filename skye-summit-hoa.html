Common questions

Phases, builders, and amenity packages use different budgets. Larger homes, golf-adjacent phases, or premium club access can carry higher assessments than entry phases.

Common area landscaping, amenity operations (pools, fitness, trails where applicable), security features, and reserve funding for future repairs.

Request recent financials, reserve study summary, community rules (community rules (CC&Rs)), and any pending extra one-time HOA fees during your review period before closing.

Many master-planned communities restrict rentals or minimum lease terms. Verify current HOA rules and financial documents for the specific subdivision.

Coverage will vary by builder and phase—often landscaping, common areas, and shared amenities. Request documents for your specific subdivision as phases release.

Rental rules will be set by each HOA as phases launch. Verify restrictions in writing before closing.

Questions to ask on tour

Monthly assessment

Current fee, what is included, and planned increases.

Reserves

Health of reserve funding for roads, walls, and amenities.

Amenity access

Which pools, gyms, or clubs your phase can use.
